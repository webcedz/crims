What is Crime?

Crime is an act or omission punishable by public laws.

There are four specific types of crime:

A. Felonies: Referes to those violations of the Revised Penal Code.
B. Offenses: Refers to those violations of statutes of Special Laws.
C. Infraction or Misdemeanor: Refers to those violations of ordinates.
D. Delinquency: Refes to those violations commited by minor offenders.

Concepts of Crime, Law, and Justice

The 1987 Philippine COnstitution provided the distribution of the great powers of the goverment its three branchhes: Executive, Legistative, and Judiciary. This three branches are co-equal and they observe separation of powers.
