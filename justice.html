JUSTICE!!

    Justice is the act of rendering what are due and treating persons equally. \ These persons must, however, fall within the same classification. (Gacayan, 2006) Essence of Justice under the Philippine Constitution.

    Under Sec. 1, Art III of the 1987 Philippine Constitution, no person shall be deprived of life, liberty or property without due process of law, nor shall a person be deprived of the equal protection of law. This guarantee dictates that in order that justice will be realized there must be the observance of due process. Due process is a guaranty against any arbitrariness on the part of the government, whether committed by the legislative, executive or the judiciary. This concept can be explored through various dimensions:

 1. Procedural Justice.

         - It focuses on the fairness of the processes by which decisions are made. According to the Procedural Justice in Policing (2019), it is the perception that legal processes are fair, unbiased, and respectful, which enhances public trust and compliance with the law.

 2. Distributive Justice.

         - It pertains to the fair allocation of resources and penalties. The Oxford Handbook of Criminal Justice (2021), defines distributive justice in criminal justice as "ensuring that the distribution of burdens and benefits, such as legal penalties and rehabilitative resources, is equitable and based on principles of fairness and equality.

 3. Restorative Justice.

         - It emphasizes repairing the harm caused by criminal behavior and fostering reconciliation between offenders and victims. It highlights that restorative justice involves "a process where all stakeholders, including victims, offenders, and the community, engage in dialogue to address the harm, seek accountability, and restore relationships.

Comparative Criminal Justice - this looks into the different model countries to investigate and evaluate a natinal system of justice in terms of criminal justice process, culture, and institution.

      - US Criminal Justice System - It is inquisitorial in nature. It is composed of three components: Law Enforcement, Courts, and Corresctions.

      - Philippine Criminal Justice System - It is an adversarial approach. The Five components are: Law Enforcement, Prosecution, Courts, Corrections, and Community-Based Programs.
