Evolution of Law

✔ Code of Hammurabi

- The first known bodies of law. Promulgated by King Hammurabi of ancient Babylon.

- This was engraved on stone tablets. It deals with the issues of theft, ownership, sexual relationships and violence.

✔ Roman Law

- Derived from Twelve Tables, a collection of basic rules related to family, religion and economic life.

✔ Common Law

- Its origin is traced from early English society.

- A body of early unwritten legal principles createdfrom social customs, rules, and practices.

✔ The Magna Carts

- Signed on June 15, 1215 by King John of England.

- It guarantees basic liberties for all British citizens.

✔ Greek Laws

- It contributes to the Western thoughts about the law coming from the ideas of the great philospher like Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle.

✔ Canon Law

- Also known as Ecclesiastical Law. Adopted for the goverment of the Christian organization and its members.

✔ Germanic Law

- A legal system that developed in the Germanic tribes of Europe.

✔ Philippine Laws

- For early Filipinos, laws are both written and unwritten. Written are those decreed and codified, while unwritten are those based on customs and traditions.

✔ Maragtas Code

- A written code in the pre-Spanish era. The oldest body of laws in the Philippines. Written by Datu Sumakwel of Panay in 1250

✔ Kalintao Code

- The second oldest legal code in the Philippines. Written by Datu Kalintao of Panay in 1433 A.D
